Willie Dixon

Willie Dixon, real name Willie James Dixon, was born July 1st, 1915 in Vicksburg, Mississippi. A songwriter, arranger, solo artist, producer, and bassist, he wrote songs for Chess Records artists including Muddy Waters, Howlin’ Wolf, Little Walter, Bo Diddley, Sonny Boy Williamson, Otis Rush, Buddy Guy, and Koko Taylor. He wrote/co-wrote over five hundred songs. “Hoochie Coochie Man”, “Spoonful”, “I’m Ready”, “Back Door Man”, “My Babe”, “Bring It On Home”, “The Seventh Son”, “I Can’t Quit You Baby”, and “Wang Dang Doodle” are all Blues standards. He’s known for saying, “Blues are the roots and the other music’s are the fruits.” His songs have been recorded by: Eric Clapton, The Doors, Led Zeppelin, Stevie Ray Vaughan, Van Morrison, The Grateful Dead, George Thorogood, Chuck Berry, The Rolling Stones, Tom Petty, Sting, and Warren Zevon. In the 1970’s and 1980’s, Dixon struggled with diabetes. In 1980, he was inducted into the Blues Hall of Fame and in 1982, he established The Blues Heaven Foundation located at 2120 South Michigan Avenue (the old Chess Records Building) to keep the Blues alive and educate people. In 1989, his album Hidden Charms won a Grammy. Willie Dixon died January 29th, 1992 in Burbank, California. The cause of death was acute circulatory collapse due to diabetes. He is buried at Burr Oak Cemetery in Alsip, Illinois. In 1994, he was inducted to The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame ‘Early Influence’ category.
